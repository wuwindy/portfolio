Design Background

The Hololens application primarily features AI and AR smart applications designed to assist new personnel in quickly understanding industrial site inspection procedures. Through this application, AI recognition facilitates rapid identification of issues, thereby resolving industrial site problems efficiently.

Role and Collaborations

As a product designer, due to the distinct usability of Hololens compared to conventional devices, we conduct minimal usability tests with users specifically for the device to understand potential operational issues users may encounter.
Additionally, given the higher learning curve of the device, we observe user behaviors and feedback continuously to inform ongoing design improvements.

Design Goal

AI Detection Addresses Human Errors

Design the procedure of AI detection identifies equipment anomalies, improper operations, or defective products, prompting real-time alerts and fix procedures.

Step-by-Step Guidance

Provide users with a certain degree of flexibility, allowing them to define header values on their own to meet their detection requirements.

Improves Work Efficiency

AR technology provides clear maps and shortest route guides for comprehensive inspection in minimal time.

User testing

User testing

Regarding feature development projects, we will first create Figma prototypes and draft a user interview plan. Based on the interview outcomes, we will conduct testing, and subsequently, usability testing of the features based on test items and results.

Design Language System

In defining the design system, the textual elements were configured with reference to the official Hololens documentation. Larger text sizes were employed for certain critical information to ensure users can easily notice important details during inspections.

Additionally, due to the ability of Hololens to trigger buttons via voice commands, in defining the system, we not only consider conventional buttons but also define buttons specifically for voice control.

Device control involves dialogs for various scenarios, we have also defined the system to address these specific contexts accordingly.

User flow

Due to ongoing functional development, we have continuously refined and defined the user flow from initial app launch to overall process planning, starting from the early design stages. This serves as a design reference and development project flow.

Procedure

In the overall application, Hololens spatial positioning will be utilized to guide users to the correct locations for initiating operational procedures.

Additionally, since operators may encounter issues during operations that cannot be resolved through routine inspection, they can also make phone calls to experts for operational guidance and instructions.

Finally, after completing the entire operational procedure, a comprehensive inspection report will also be provided, allowing operators to review the content and results of the inspection.

Take away

Due to the current steep learning curve for users with smart wearable devices, continuous gathering of user feedback and conducting user testing are necessary to achieve better design outcomes.

Continuous collection of user feedback, discussions with engineers on corresponding solutions, and attention to reference information provided by device manufacturers are believed to facilitate ongoing optimization and iteration of smart wearable device designs.
